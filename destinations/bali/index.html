Why Influencers Love Bali

The island that 2,400+ travel creators cannot stop filming

Bali is not just a destination ‚Äî it is a phenomenon. No other place on Earth has been featured by more travel influencers, and the reasons are as layered as the island's ancient terraced rice paddies. At its core, Bali offers something almost impossible to find elsewhere: affordable luxury in a setting so photogenic that every angle looks like a professionally art-directed campaign. A private villa with an infinity pool overlooking a jungle valley costs less per night than a standard hotel room in most Western capitals. That economic reality alone has made Bali the creative home base for thousands of content creators.

But affordability is only part of the equation. Bali is staggeringly beautiful in a way that translates effortlessly to camera. The emerald cascade of the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, the mist-wrapped temples of Ubud, the impossibly blue waters of Nusa Penida, the golden-hour glow over Uluwatu's clifftop temple ‚Äî these are scenes that stop a viewer mid-scroll. The island packs an extraordinary diversity of landscapes into an area smaller than most national parks: volcanic peaks, crater lakes, ancient forests, black-sand beaches, turquoise coves, and some of the most dramatic sea cliffs in the world.

Bali's spiritual culture adds a depth that purely scenic destinations cannot match. The island is home to over 20,000 Hindu temples, and daily offerings of flowers, incense, and rice ‚Äî called canang sari ‚Äî line every doorstep, street corner, and beach. Sacred water purification ceremonies at Tirta Empul, the hypnotic Kecak fire dance at Uluwatu, and the silent stillness of Nyepi (the Balinese Day of Silence) offer creators moments of genuine transcendence that resonate powerfully with audiences seeking meaning beyond the surface.

The island has also become the undisputed capital of the digital nomad movement. Canggu, once a sleepy rice-farming village, is now a buzzing hub of co-working spaces, specialty coffee shops, and surf breaks where laptop entrepreneurs and full-time creators share tables. The community effect is self-reinforcing: creators come for the content, stay for the lifestyle, and their content draws more creators. Seminyak offers a more polished, boutique-hotel energy, while Ubud remains the spiritual and artistic heart ‚Äî a place where yoga retreats, vegan cafes, and traditional Balinese art galleries coexist in lush harmony. Whether you are a budget backpacker or a luxury seeker, a surfer or a meditator, a foodie or an adventurer, Bali has a version of itself waiting for you. That universal appeal is why it stands alone at the top of the creator leaderboard.

Must-Visit Locations

Each region of Bali offers a completely different experience

Ubud

The cultural and spiritual heart of Bali. Home to the Sacred Monkey Forest, Tegallalang Rice Terraces, world-class yoga retreats, traditional art markets, and lush jungle valleys. The soul of the island.

The most iconic rice terrace landscape in all of Southeast Asia. Dramatic cascading paddies carved into the hillside using the ancient Subak irrigation system ‚Äî a UNESCO-recognized cultural landscape.

Best Time to Visit

Bali is a year-round destination, but timing matters

Dry Season (Apr‚ÄìOct)

The most popular time to visit. Sunny skies, low humidity, and temperatures around 27-30 degrees Celsius. July and August are peak season with the highest prices and most crowds. June and September are the sweet spot ‚Äî great weather with thinner crowds.

Wet Season (Nov‚ÄìMar)

Afternoon tropical downpours, but mornings are often sunny and clear. The landscape is lush and brilliantly green. Hotel prices drop 30-50%, and popular sites have far fewer tourists. Surfers love the wet season swell on the east coast.

Creator Tip

Most travel creators recommend April-May or September-October as the ideal months. You get dry-season weather, shoulder-season prices, and the best light for photography ‚Äî golden hours are longer, and the rice terraces are at their greenest after the wet season.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before visiting Bali

What is the best time to visit Bali?

The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season from April to October. June through September offers the most reliably sunny weather with low humidity and temperatures around 27-30 degrees Celsius. July and August are peak tourist season with higher prices. The shoulder months of April-May and September-October provide excellent weather with fewer crowds and better hotel deals. The wet season (November-March) brings afternoon downpours but also the lushest green landscapes and the lowest prices.

How much does a trip to Bali cost?

Bali is one of the most affordable luxury destinations in the world. Budget travelers can enjoy Bali on $30-50 USD per day including accommodation, food, and transport. Mid-range travelers spend $80-150 per day with boutique hotels and nicer restaurants. Luxury travelers can enjoy private villas with infinity pools, fine dining, and premium experiences for $200-500+ per day ‚Äî a fraction of comparable luxury elsewhere. A private villa with a pool often costs less than a basic hotel room in Europe. The Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) makes Western currencies go very far.

Do I need a visa to visit Bali?

Most nationalities can obtain a Visa on Arrival (VOA) at Ngurah Rai International Airport for 500,000 IDR (approximately $30 USD), valid for 30 days and extendable once for another 30 days. Citizens of many ASEAN countries can enter visa-free for 30 days. For longer stays, a B211A social visa allows 60 days with extensions up to 180 days. Digital nomads should look into Indonesia's remote worker visa program. Always check the latest requirements with the Indonesian embassy before traveling.

Is Bali safe for tourists?

Bali is generally very safe for tourists and is one of the safest destinations in Southeast Asia. The Balinese people are exceptionally warm and welcoming. Use common-sense precautions: choose reputable transport, be cautious with motorbike rentals if you are inexperienced, watch for strong ocean currents at certain beaches, stay hydrated, and be mindful of belongings in crowded tourist areas. The tap water is not safe to drink ‚Äî stick to bottled or filtered water. Travel insurance is highly recommended.

What are the must-see spots in Bali?

The absolute must-see spots include the Tegallalang Rice Terraces near Ubud, the cliff-top Uluwatu Temple at sunset with its Kecak fire dance, the sacred water temple of Tirta Empul, the dramatic Kelingking Beach on Nusa Penida (the T-Rex cliff), the Handara Gate in the northern highlands, and the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary in Ubud. For beaches, do not miss Padang Padang, Nyang Nyang, and the black sand beaches of the north coast. For waterfalls, Sekumpul and Tegenungan are essential.
