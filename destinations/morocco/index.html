Why Creators Love Morocco

The kingdom that 700+ travel creators cannot stop filming

Morocco is not simply a destination — it is an all-out sensory assault in the best possible way. Step into the medina of Marrakech and your senses are immediately overwhelmed: the call to prayer echoing across terracotta rooftops, the scent of cumin and saffron drifting from spice stalls, the riot of hand-dyed leather goods and handwoven carpets stacked floor to ceiling, the sizzle of lamb kebabs on charcoal grills, and the kaleidoscopic mosaic of zellige tilework adorning every riad doorway. For content creators, Morocco offers something rare — a place so visually dense that every single frame tells a story without a word of narration.

The Sahara Desert is the headline act that draws creators from across the globe. A night spent in a luxury desert camp among the towering dunes of Erg Chebbi near Merzouga is one of travel's most transformative experiences — camel treks into a sunset that paints the sand in shades of gold, crimson, and violet, followed by Berber drumming under a canopy of stars so vivid they look artificial. The footage is breathtaking. But Morocco is so much more than the desert. The blue-washed city of Chefchaouen, nestled in the Rif Mountains, delivers one of the most photographed streetscapes on Earth — every wall, staircase, and doorway is painted in shades of cobalt, powder blue, and indigo, creating a dreamlike world that seems designed specifically for cameras.

Then there is the food. Moroccan cuisine is among the most celebrated in the world, and creators specializing in food content find an inexhaustible well of material: slow-cooked tagines with preserved lemons and olives, fluffy couscous served on communal platters, freshly baked msemen flatbread drizzled with honey, the legendary street food stalls of Jemaa el-Fna square in Marrakech, and the ritual of traditional mint tea poured from a height into ornate glasses. The ancient medinas of Fez — the oldest continuously inhabited medieval city in the world — offer a labyrinth of 9,000 alleyways where tanneries, brass workshops, and carpet looms have operated for over a thousand years. Morocco delivers a culture clash that is vivid, photogenic, and endlessly rewarding for any creator willing to explore its layers.

Must-Visit Locations

Each region of Morocco offers a completely different world

Marrakech

The Red City. Home to the legendary Jemaa el-Fna square, sprawling souks, the stunning Bahia Palace, Majorelle Garden, and some of the most beautiful riads in the world. The sensory epicenter of Morocco.

Must-Visit

Fez

The oldest continuously inhabited medieval city in the world. Its medina contains 9,000 alleyways, the legendary Chouara Tannery, Al-Qarawiyyin University (the oldest in the world), and centuries-old artisan workshops.

Cultural Capital

Chefchaouen

The Blue Pearl of Morocco. Nestled in the Rif Mountains, this dreamy city features blue-washed buildings at every turn, creating one of the most photographed streetscapes on the planet. A photographer's paradise.

Photography

Sahara / Merzouga

The gateway to the Erg Chebbi dunes — towering 150 meters of golden sand. Camel treks at sunset, luxury desert camps under the Milky Way, and sunrise that sets the dunes ablaze in amber and rose.

Adventure

Essaouira

A windswept Atlantic coastal town beloved by surfers, artists, and creators seeking a laid-back escape. Whitewashed ramparts, a vibrant fishing port, world-class windsurfing, and a thriving art scene.

Coastal Escape

Atlas Mountains

North Africa's greatest mountain range. Trek to the summit of Jebel Toubkal (4,167 m), explore the dramatic Todra and Dades gorges, and visit remote Berber villages unchanged for centuries.

Trekking

Top Activities

The experiences that creators film most in Morocco

Souk Shopping

Navigate the labyrinthine souks of Marrakech and Fez — handwoven carpets, leather goods, brass lanterns, spices, ceramics, and textiles. Haggling is an art form and part of the cultural experience.

Must-Do Varies

Camel Trekking

Ride dromedary camels into the Erg Chebbi dunes of the Sahara at sunset. Multi-day treks include overnight stays in Berber camps with traditional music, feasts, and stargazing in total silence.

Adventure From $50

Hammam Spa

Experience a traditional Moroccan hammam — a centuries-old bathing ritual involving steam, black soap, vigorous exfoliation with a kessa glove, and argan oil massage. Deeply cleansing and culturally immersive.

Wellness $10–80

Cooking Class

Learn to prepare authentic tagines, couscous, pastilla, and harira in a traditional Moroccan kitchen. Classes typically begin with a guided market tour to select fresh spices and ingredients.

Food & Culture $30–80

Desert Camping

Spend a night in a luxury desert camp among the Sahara dunes — private tents with real beds, candlelit dinners, Berber drumming, and a sky full of stars you will never forget. A transformative experience.

Bucket List $80–400

Surfing Essaouira

Ride Atlantic waves at Essaouira and nearby Sidi Kaouki. Consistent swells, warm water, and a laid-back surf culture make this a rising star on the global surf scene. Lessons available for all levels.

Water Sports $15–50

Best Time to Visit

Morocco is a year-round destination with distinct seasonal advantages

Spring (Mar–May)

The ideal time to visit. Comfortable temperatures of 20-28 degrees Celsius, wildflowers blooming in the Atlas Mountains, and lush green landscapes after winter rains. Shoulder-season pricing with thinner crowds at major attractions. Perfect for both desert and mountain excursions.

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

Equally excellent conditions. Summer heat subsides to pleasant warmth, the Sahara becomes comfortable for overnight camping, and the Atlantic coast is still warm enough for surfing. Festival season brings cultural events across the country.

Creator Tip

Most travel creators recommend April or October as the sweet spot — warm but not scorching, golden light for photography, and far fewer tourists than peak season. For the Sahara, October through March offers the most comfortable desert temperatures. Avoid interior cities in July and August when temperatures can exceed 40 degrees Celsius.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before visiting Morocco

What is the best time to visit Morocco?

The best time to visit Morocco is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). These shoulder seasons offer comfortable temperatures between 20-28 degrees Celsius, clear skies, and fewer crowds. Summer brings intense heat to interior cities like Marrakech and Fez, though coastal Essaouira stays pleasant. Winter is ideal for the Sahara and southern regions but can be cold in the Atlas Mountains. April and October are widely considered the sweet spot for photography, comfortable sightseeing, and favorable pricing.

Is Morocco safe for tourists?

Morocco is generally very safe for tourists and is one of the most visited countries in Africa, welcoming over 14 million visitors annually. Moroccans are famously hospitable. Use common-sense precautions: be aware of persistent touts in tourist areas, negotiate prices before purchasing in souks, use licensed guides for desert and mountain excursions, and keep valuables secure in crowded medinas. Solo female travelers should dress modestly in more traditional areas. The tourist police maintain a visible presence in major cities.

How much does a trip to Morocco cost?

Morocco offers excellent value at every budget level. Budget travelers can enjoy Morocco on $30-50 USD per day with guesthouse accommodation, street food, and shared transport. Mid-range travelers spend $80-150 per day staying in beautiful riads, eating at restaurants, and taking guided tours. Luxury travelers can experience palatial riads, private desert camps, and fine dining for $250-600+ per day. A stunning traditional riad in the medina often costs a fraction of a comparable boutique hotel in Europe. The Moroccan Dirham (MAD) provides strong purchasing power for most Western currencies.

Do I need a visa to visit Morocco?

Citizens of many countries including the USA, UK, EU nations, Canada, and Australia can enter Morocco visa-free for up to 90 days. You will need a passport valid for at least six months beyond your entry date. No visa application or fees are required — simply arrive at the airport or border crossing with your passport. For stays longer than 90 days, you must apply for an extension at a local police station. Always check the latest requirements with the Moroccan embassy or consulate before traveling.
