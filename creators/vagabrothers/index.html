About the Vagabrothers

Marko and Alex Ayling are the Vagabrothers, a sibling duo who have turned their lifelong love of travel and filmmaking into one of YouTube's most visually stunning travel channels. As dual American-German citizens who grew up bilingual, they developed an appreciation for cross-cultural connection long before they ever picked up a camera.

Both brothers studied film, and it shows in every frame of their content. Their city guides are not typical tourist rundowns -- they are carefully crafted cinematic experiences that blend documentary storytelling with practical travel advice. From the neon-lit streets of Tokyo to the sun-soaked plazas of Barcelona, each video immerses viewers in the rhythm, culture, and soul of a destination.

With over 1.5 million subscribers and 90+ countries under their belts, the Vagabrothers have established themselves as the gold standard for cinematic travel content on YouTube. Their work goes beyond surface-level tourism, diving deep into local culture, cuisine, history, and the human stories that make each destination unique.

Their signature format -- the 48-hour city guide -- has become a benchmark in the travel content world. These videos distill the essence of a city into a tightly edited, beautifully shot narrative that covers must-see landmarks, hidden local gems, the best food spots, and practical tips for getting around. Whether it is navigating the labyrinthine alleys of Marrakech or riding the metro in Tokyo, every guide balances aspiration with accessibility, helping viewers plan trips they can actually take.

Beyond their YouTube presence, the Vagabrothers have collaborated with tourism boards, airlines, and hospitality brands across the globe. Yet they have maintained editorial independence, always prioritizing honest, experience-driven recommendations over sponsored talking points. Their audience trusts them precisely because authenticity has never been negotiable.

What truly sets them apart is their commitment to sustainable and responsible travel. They advocate for slow travel, supporting local economies, and minimizing environmental impact. For Marko and Alex, meaningful travel is not about checking boxes on a bucket list -- it is about forging genuine connections with the places and people they encounter along the way.

The Journey

From a bilingual childhood to becoming YouTube's premier cinematic travel storytellers.

Childhood - USA & Germany
Growing Up Bilingual
Marko and Alex grew up as dual American-German citizens in a bilingual household. Exposure to two cultures from birth sparked an insatiable curiosity about the wider world and planted the seeds for a life of travel.
University Years
Film School & Finding Their Voice
Both brothers pursued film studies, learning the art of visual storytelling, cinematography, and documentary production. These skills would later become the foundation of their distinctive cinematic travel style.
2013 - YouTube
Launching the Channel
The Vagabrothers YouTube channel was born, combining their filmmaking expertise with a passion for authentic travel. Early videos showcased a polished, narrative-driven approach that stood out from typical travel vlogs.
2016 - Europe
City Guide Series Takes Off
Their signature 48-hour city guide format gained massive traction. Barcelona, Lisbon, and Amsterdam guides became go-to resources for travelers, earning millions of views and establishing the brothers as city guide authorities.
2019 - Global
1 Million Subscribers
Crossed the 1 million subscriber milestone. Content expanded to cover destinations across Asia, Latin America, and beyond, while maintaining the cinematic quality and cultural depth that defined their brand.
2022-Present
1.5M+ and Sustainability Advocacy
Surpassed 1.5 million subscribers with 90+ countries visited. Became vocal advocates for sustainable travel, encouraging slow travel and local engagement while continuing to produce award-worthy cinematic guides.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Vagabrothers' travel videos so cinematic?
Marko and Alex both studied film, which gives their travel content a distinctly cinematic quality. They use professional-grade cameras, carefully composed shots, color grading, and narrative storytelling techniques borrowed from documentary filmmaking. Their city guides feel more like short films than typical vlogs, with attention to pacing, music selection, and visual transitions that immerse viewers in each destination.
How do the Vagabrothers plan their travel itineraries?
The Vagabrothers recommend a mix of research and spontaneity. They typically outline key experiences and cultural highlights before arriving, but leave room for serendipitous discoveries. They prioritize local recommendations over tourist traps, seek out neighborhood eateries over chain restaurants, and always try to connect with locals for authentic insider tips. Their 48-hour city guide format is designed to help travelers maximize limited time.
Are the Vagabrothers actually brothers?
Yes, Marko and Alex Ayling are real brothers. They grew up in a bilingual household as dual American-German citizens, which gave them an early appreciation for different cultures and languages. Their sibling dynamic and shared passion for travel and filmmaking give their content an authentic, natural chemistry that resonates with viewers worldwide.
What is the Vagabrothers' approach to sustainable travel?
The Vagabrothers are vocal advocates for sustainable and responsible travel. They encourage slow travel over rushing through destinations, support local businesses and economies, highlight eco-friendly accommodations and transport options, and educate their audience about minimizing their environmental footprint while traveling. They believe that meaningful travel should benefit both the traveler and the communities they visit.
