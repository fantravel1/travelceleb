The Origin Story

Born in Poland, raised in Dubai, educated in the UK. Eva zu Beck had the kind of life most people dream about‚Äîa high-paying finance job in one of the world's most glamorous cities, a comfortable apartment, and a clear career trajectory. But something was missing.

In 2017, she made a decision that would change everything. She quit her corporate finance job, sold her belongings, and bought a one-way ticket to India. What started as a gap year turned into a complete life transformation.

Unlike most travel influencers who stick to well-trodden paths, Eva deliberately sought out the places others feared to go. Pakistan. Afghanistan. Papua New Guinea. Iran. Saudi Arabia. Her mission: to show the world that these "dangerous" destinations are filled with incredible hospitality, rich culture, and breathtaking beauty.

Her Pakistan series in 2018-2019 changed the game. At a time when Western media portrayed Pakistan as nothing but terrorism and conflict, Eva showed millions of viewers the stunning Hunza Valley, the warmth of Pakistani hospitality, and the incredible safety she experienced as a solo female traveler. The Pakistani government even recognized her contribution to tourism.

This page maps her entire journey‚Äîevery destination, with tips, tricks, and insider advice you won't find anywhere else.

Eva's Solo Female Travel Philosophy

üåç "Dangerous" is Relative

The places Western media calls dangerous often have the warmest hospitality. Pakistan was safer than many European cities. Research beyond headlines‚Äîtalk to travelers who've actually been there.

üëó Dress for Respect

In conservative countries, dressing modestly isn't oppression‚Äîit's respect. It also makes you less visible, more approachable, and opens doors that wouldn't open otherwise.

üí¨ Learn Local Phrases

Even "hello," "thank you," and "delicious" in the local language transforms interactions. People light up when you try. It shows respect and builds instant connection.

üè† Stay with Locals

Couchsurfing, homestays, and local guesthouses give you protection, insight, and friendship that hotels never can. Families often become the most memorable part of any trip.

üì± Share Your Location

Always have someone who knows your itinerary. Share live location with family. Check in regularly. Freedom and responsibility go together.

üéí Trust Your Instincts

If something feels wrong, leave. No photo is worth your safety. But also: don't let fear of "what ifs" stop you from experiencing the world's incredible diversity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Eva afford to travel full-time?
Eva earns through YouTube ad revenue, brand partnerships, speaking engagements, and tourism board collaborations. Her Pakistan content notably led to work with Pakistan's tourism sector. She's also built courses and consulted for travel companies.
Is it really safe for women to travel to Pakistan alone?
According to Eva, she felt safer in Pakistan than in many Western countries. The culture has strong protections for guests and women travelers often receive extra hospitality. She recommends dressing modestly, using common sense, and sticking to tourist-friendly areas in the north. The Hunza Valley in particular has a long history of welcoming solo female travelers.
What camera gear does Eva use?
Eva has used various setups over the years. Her main cameras have included Sony a7 series for video and a DJI drone for aerials. She emphasizes that content matters more than gear‚Äîher early viral videos were shot on basic equipment. For beginners, she recommends starting with whatever you have.
What's Eva's most controversial trip and why?
Her Afghanistan trip in 2019 drew the most debate. Critics questioned whether it was responsible to show the country as tourist-friendly given security concerns. Eva argued that showing positive stories from Afghanistan helped the local tourism industry and provided a counter-narrative to constant war coverage. She always noted the risks and recommended thorough research.
How can I start traveling to unconventional destinations?
Eva's advice: Start with a "gateway" destination. If you want to visit Pakistan, try Iran or Nepal first to build confidence. Join travel groups on Facebook for recent reports. Connect with local guides before you go. Start with shorter trips. And most importantly‚Äîdon't let fear-based media dictate your travel choices. Research, prepare, and go.
