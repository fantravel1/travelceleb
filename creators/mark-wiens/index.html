About Mark Wiens

The man behind the world's most iconic food reaction face

Mark Wiens is widely regarded as the world's foremost food travel content creator, having built a YouTube empire of over 10 million subscribers who follow his adventures eating authentic, local cuisine across every corner of the globe. Born in Phoenix, Arizona, Mark's multicultural upbringing took him from the deserts of the American Southwest to the lush landscapes of the Democratic Republic of Congo and the rolling countryside of France, instilling in him a deep curiosity for diverse cultures and, above all, their food.

After graduating from Arizona State University, Mark moved to Bangkok, Thailand in 2009, where he launched his food blog, Migrationology.com, documenting the vibrant street food scene of Southeast Asia. What started as a passion project quickly grew into something much larger. Mark's enthusiasm for food was infectious -- his now-legendary reaction face, a wide-eyed expression of pure bliss upon tasting an incredible dish, became one of the most recognizable trademarks on YouTube. It was not a performance; it was genuine joy, and viewers around the world could feel it through the screen.

Mark's YouTube channel grew steadily and then explosively, fueled by his deep dives into street food cultures that most Western audiences had never seen. His Bangkok street food series became a phenomenon, introducing millions to the flavors of pad thai cooked in a fiery wok by the side of a busy road, the perfect balance of a freshly pounded som tam (papaya salad), and the heavenly sweetness of mango sticky rice. But Thailand was just the beginning. His Indian street food series captivated audiences with tours through the vibrant markets of Mumbai, Delhi, and Kolkata, while his Japanese food guides revealed the precision and artistry of ramen, sushi, and kaiseki cuisine.

Today, Mark lives in Bangkok with his wife Ying (Yingluck), who is Thai and a frequent presence in his videos, and their son Micah. Together, the family has traveled to over 80 countries, documenting food cultures from Mexico City's taco stands to Istanbul's kebab shops, from Marrakech's spice-laden souks to Lima's world-renowned cevicherias. Mark has authored bestselling books on food travel and has been featured in major media outlets worldwide. His authenticity remains his greatest asset -- Mark does not chase trends or sponsor deals at the expense of genuine exploration. Every bite he takes, every market he visits, and every recipe he uncovers comes from a place of real passion and love for food and the people who make it.

The Food Journey

How Mark Wiens became the world's biggest food travel creator

Early Years
A Multicultural Childhood
Born in Phoenix, Arizona, Mark grew up in France and the Democratic Republic of Congo, developing a deep appreciation for diverse cultures and cuisines from an early age.
2009
Migrationology is Born
After moving to Bangkok, Thailand, Mark launches migrationology.com -- a food and travel blog that would become the foundation of his digital empire.
2010 - 2012
YouTube Beginnings
Mark starts uploading food videos to YouTube, documenting Bangkok's vibrant street food scene. His authentic enthusiasm and detailed reviews start gaining a loyal following.
2013 - 2014
The Thai Street Food Explosion
His Bangkok street food guides go viral, introducing millions to som tam, pad thai, and the magic of Thai street cooking. The iconic reaction face becomes his trademark.
2015 - 2016
Going Global
Mark expands beyond Southeast Asia, filming epic food tours in India, the Middle East, and Africa. His Indian street food series becomes a massive hit, crossing 50 million views.
2017 - 2018
Crossing 5 Million Subscribers
The channel hits a major milestone. Mark and Ying welcome their son Micah, who quickly becomes a fan favorite. The family travels together, introducing food to a new generation.
2019 - 2021
Books, Media & 10M Milestone
Mark publishes bestselling food travel books, is featured in major media outlets, and crosses 10 million subscribers. Despite the pandemic, he continues exploring Thai food in incredible depth.
2022 - Present
The Food Legend Continues
With over 3 billion total views, Mark continues pushing boundaries -- filming in lesser-known food destinations, collaborating with local chefs, and inspiring millions to explore the world through food.
