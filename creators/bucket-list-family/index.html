The Story of The Bucket List Family

From selling everything on Craigslist to showing three kids every corner of the world -- the most inspiring family travel story on the internet.

In 2015, Garrett and Jessica Gee made a decision that most parents would consider unthinkable: they sold their house, their cars, and nearly everything they owned to travel the world full-time with their young children. What started as an ambitious family experiment has become one of the most beloved travel stories on the internet, inspiring millions of families to dream bigger.

The story begins with Garrett's entrepreneurial success. While still in college, he created Scan, a QR code scanning application that became one of the most downloaded apps in the world. When Snapchat acquired Scan, Garrett and Jessica suddenly had the financial freedom to pursue a dream they had talked about since their early days of dating: seeing the entire world together as a family.

Rather than settling into comfortable suburban life, the Gees sold everything on Craigslist and KSL Classifieds. With their daughter Dorothy still a toddler, they boarded a one-way flight and began what would become a multi-year journey across more than 85 countries on all seven continents. Their family has since grown to include Manilla and Calihan, both of whom have known the travel lifestyle since birth.

What makes The Bucket List Family truly special is their emphasis on family values and togetherness. Every adventure is viewed through the lens of parenthood. Their content demonstrates that having kids is not a barrier to adventure but rather an enhancement of it. Watching their children interact with different cultures, learn new languages organically, and develop confidence in unfamiliar environments has inspired millions of families worldwide to rethink what is possible.

The Journey Timeline

From selling everything on Craigslist to seeing every corner of the world as a family of five.

2015 -- Utah
Sold Everything to Travel the World
After Garrett's app Scan was acquired by Snapchat, the Gees sold their home, cars, and nearly all their belongings. With toddler Dorothy in tow, they boarded a one-way flight and never looked back. The family chose experiences over possessions -- a decision that would inspire millions.
2016 -- Tonga, South Pacific
Swimming with Humpback Whales
The family swam alongside humpback whales in Tonga's crystal-clear waters, creating what would become their most iconic and emotionally powerful video. This single experience -- a mother whale and her calf gliding past the Gee children -- captured the heart of the internet and defined their brand forever.
2017 -- Worldwide
1 Million Subscribers
The channel surpassed one million subscribers as the family traveled through Europe and beyond. Son Manilla was born during this period, making the Gees a family of four on the road. Their honest portrayal of traveling with a newborn resonated with young parents everywhere.
2018 -- The Amazing Race
Won $100,000 on Reality TV
Garrett and Jessica competed on The Amazing Race, winning $100,000 and introducing The Bucket List Family to a massive new television audience. Their competitive spirit and teamwork on the show mirrored the partnership that makes their family travel possible.
2019 -- Family of Five
Calihan Joins the Adventure
The family welcomed their third child, Calihan, proving once again that growing a family and traveling the world are not mutually exclusive. The children continued their worldschooling education across Southeast Asia, learning through firsthand cultural immersion rather than textbooks.
2021 -- Hawaii
Established a Home Base
After years of non-stop travel, the family set up a home base in Hawaii -- the perfect launchpad for Pacific adventures while giving the kids a sense of stability. They balanced schooling, community, and extended travel trips throughout the year.
2023 -- Present
85+ Countries and Counting
With over 85 countries visited across all seven continents, the family shows no signs of slowing down. From luxury resorts in the Maldives to rugged adventures in New Zealand, they continue to prove that the greatest gift you can give your children is not a bigger house but a bigger world.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you want to know about The Bucket List Family's travel style and adventures.

Who is The Bucket List Family?
The Bucket List Family consists of Garrett and Jessica Gee and their three children: Dorothy, Manilla, and Calihan. In 2015, they sold everything they owned -- house, cars, belongings -- and set off to travel the world full-time as a family. Garrett previously created the QR code app Scan, which was acquired by Snapchat, giving the family the financial foundation to pursue their dream of showing their kids the world.
How does The Bucket List Family afford to travel?
Their initial travel fund came from the sale of Garrett's app Scan to Snapchat. They also won $100,000 on The Amazing Race. Today, the family earns through YouTube ad revenue (3M+ subscribers, 500M+ total views), brand partnerships with luxury resorts and travel companies, social media sponsorships across Instagram and TikTok, and various business ventures. They have been transparent about earning a full-time living through content creation.
How do the kids get educated while traveling?
The Gee children follow a worldschooling approach, learning through real-world experiences rather than traditional classroom settings. Swimming with marine biologists in Tonga becomes a science lesson. Exploring ancient temples teaches history. Navigating foreign markets builds math skills. The family supplements hands-on learning with structured homeschool curriculum, and now that they have a home base in Hawaii, the children also attend local school during non-travel periods.
What are The Bucket List Family's most iconic adventures?
Their most iconic moments include swimming with humpback whales in Tonga, buying a private island, living in a treehouse in Bali, camping under the Northern Lights in Iceland, exploring overwater villas in the Maldives with the kids, and volcano surfing in Nicaragua. The whale swimming video in particular became a viral sensation and is often cited as one of the most beautiful travel moments ever captured on YouTube.
