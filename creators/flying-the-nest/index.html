The Flying The Nest Journey

From leaving Australia to building a global family travel channel -- the milestones that shaped Stephen and Jess's story.

2015
Leaving Australia -- The Adventure Begins
Stephen and Jess made the life-changing decision to leave the comfort of Australia and set off to see the world. With cameras in hand and a desire to document everything, they sold their belongings, said goodbye to the familiar, and boarded a one-way flight to Southeast Asia. It was a leap of faith that would become the foundation of Flying The Nest.
2016
Starting YouTube -- Finding Their Voice
What began as casual travel footage quickly evolved into a dedicated YouTube channel. Stephen's natural talent for cinematography set their content apart from the start. They developed a signature style blending cinematic visuals with honest, down-to-earth storytelling. Early videos from Bali and Thailand gained traction as viewers connected with their authentic approach and stunning production quality.
2019 - 2021
Growing Family -- A New Chapter
The arrival of their first child transformed Flying The Nest in the most beautiful way. Rather than slowing down, Stephen and Jess embraced family travel wholeheartedly. They shared the honest realities of traveling with a baby -- the sleepless nights, the logistical challenges, and the indescribable joy of watching their child experience the world. Their content resonated deeply with young families around the globe.
2022 - Present
Moving to Portugal -- A European Base
In a move that excited their audience, Stephen and Jess relocated from Australia to Portugal. The decision gave them a strategic European base, making it easier to explore the continent while offering their family a rich cultural experience. They documented the entire relocation journey, from visa applications to finding a home, providing a real-world guide for others considering a similar international move.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Flying The Nest, their travel style, and family adventures.

How does Flying The Nest manage family travel with young children?
Stephen and Jess have developed a flexible travel style that adapts to the needs of their growing family. They plan trips with slower itineraries, choose family-friendly accommodations, and build in plenty of downtime between activities. They are transparent about the challenges of traveling with kids, from dealing with jet lag to navigating airports with strollers and car seats. Their practical tips and honest approach have made them a trusted resource for families looking to explore the world without waiting until the kids are older.
What camera gear does Flying The Nest use for their cinematic vlogs?
Flying The Nest is widely recognized for their exceptionally high production quality. Stephen uses professional-grade mirrorless cameras, gimbals for smooth stabilized footage, and DJI drones for breathtaking aerial shots. Their editing style features color grading, cinematic transitions, and carefully selected music that elevates the viewing experience. They have shared detailed gear breakdowns on their channel, and Stephen has discussed how their setup has evolved from basic consumer cameras to the professional rig they use today.
Why did Flying The Nest move from Australia to Portugal?
Stephen and Jess relocated from Australia to Portugal for several compelling reasons. Portugal's central location in Europe makes it an ideal base for exploring the continent, with affordable flights to dozens of destinations. The cost of living is significantly lower than Australia, the climate is warm and pleasant year-round, and there is a thriving expat community. They also wanted to give their family the experience of living in a different culture. The entire relocation process was documented on their channel, providing invaluable insights for anyone considering a similar move.
Are Flying The Nest travel reviews honest and unbiased?
Honesty is one of the core values that sets Flying The Nest apart. Stephen and Jess are known for sharing both the highlights and the drawbacks of every destination, hotel, and experience they feature. They clearly disclose sponsored content and have turned down partnerships that did not align with their values. Their audience trusts them precisely because they do not sugarcoat their experiences. If a popular destination is overhyped or a hotel does not live up to expectations, they will say so. This integrity has been central to building their loyal community of 800K+ subscribers.
