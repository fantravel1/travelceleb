The Story of Nas Daily

From a Harvard dorm room to billions of views -- the man who proved every story can be told in one minute.

Nuseir Yassin was born in Arraba, a small Arab town in northern Israel. Growing up as an Israeli-Palestinian, he straddled two worlds from the very beginning -- an experience that would later fuel his obsession with understanding people across cultures, borders, and languages. He earned a spot at Harvard University, where he studied economics and computer science, graduating into a coveted position at Venmo, the fintech company reshaping how Americans handle money.

By all conventional measures, Nuseir had made it. But something gnawed at him. The cubicle walls felt like a cage, and the routine of coding in an office couldn't compete with the questions he kept asking himself: What does the world actually look like? Who are the people living in it? What are their stories? In 2016, he made a decision that shocked his family and colleagues -- he quit his six-figure tech job and picked up a camera. The plan was audacious and almost absurd in its simplicity: make one video every single day, each exactly one minute long, and never miss a day.

"That's one minute. See you tomorrow."
-- The closing line of every Nas Daily video

Day 1 was rough. Day 10 was harder. But by Day 100, something extraordinary was happening. Nas Daily's Facebook page was exploding. His unique format -- a tightly edited, fast-paced, one-minute cultural story with bold text overlays and an infectious energy -- was unlike anything the platform had seen. He wasn't filming tourist attractions or luxury resorts. He was filming people: a deaf barista in Malaysia, a trash collector who became a millionaire in Brazil, a village in Japan where people live past 100. Each video distilled a complex human story into 60 seconds of pure emotional impact.

Over the course of 1,000 consecutive days, Nas traveled to 64 countries, amassing over 40 million followers on Facebook and building one of the most-watched personal brands in digital media. His videos collectively racked up billions of views. When Day 1,000 arrived, it wasn't just the end of a challenge -- it was the birth of a media empire. Nuseir channeled everything he learned into Nas Academy, an online education platform designed to make knowledge as accessible and digestible as his one-minute videos.

Signature Countries

The destinations that shaped Nas Daily's most memorable stories and resonated with millions of viewers worldwide.

Israel

Nas's homeland and the starting point of his identity-driven storytelling. His videos about life as an Israeli-Palestinian offered a rare, personal perspective on one of the world's most complex regions.

Philippines

A country Nas fell deeply in love with. His videos about Filipino resilience, warmth, and community spirit became some of his most-shared content, earning a massive Filipino fanbase.

Japan

From the centenarians of Okinawa to the precision of Tokyo's train system, Nas captured Japan's fascinating blend of ancient tradition and futuristic innovation in unforgettable one-minute stories.

Iceland

Nas documented Iceland's otherworldly landscapes and progressive society, from geothermal energy powering the entire country to the world's most peaceful nation's approach to crime and equality.

Singapore

Nas eventually made Singapore his home base. His videos about the city-state's efficiency, multiculturalism, and hawker food culture turned Singapore into a recurring character in his content universe.

Tanzania

Nas's Tanzania stories highlighted human perseverance -- from Maasai communities preserving their heritage to entrepreneurs building businesses in Dar es Salaam. Africa's beauty told in 60-second bursts.

The 1,000-Day Journey

From Harvard lecture halls to a global media empire built one minute at a time. The key milestones of Nas Daily's extraordinary path.

2014 - Harvard University
Graduating from Harvard
Nuseir Yassin graduated from Harvard with a degree in economics and computer science. As an Israeli-Palestinian navigating one of the world's most elite institutions, the seed of cross-cultural storytelling was already planted.
2014-2016 - New York City
Working in Tech at Venmo
Nuseir landed a software engineering job at Venmo, earning a six-figure salary in New York. But the comfort of tech couldn't silence the restless voice telling him there were stories out in the world that needed telling.
2016 - Day 1
The First One-Minute Video
Nuseir quit his job and uploaded his very first one-minute video. The rules were simple: one video, every day, exactly one minute, no exceptions. The 1,000-day countdown began. Nobody knew it would change everything.
2017 - Day 500
Halfway Point: Going Viral
By Day 500, Nas Daily had become a Facebook phenomenon. Millions of followers tuned in daily. The one-minute format had proven itself: concise, emotional, and infinitely shareable. He had visited over 30 countries and showed no signs of slowing down.
2019 - Day 1,000
The Final Video: Mission Complete
One thousand consecutive days. Sixty-four countries. Billions of views. Nas uploaded his final daily video to an audience of tens of millions, closing the most ambitious personal video project in social media history. The sign-off was the same as always: "That's one minute. See you tomorrow." Except this time, there was no tomorrow.
2020-Present
Building Nas Academy
With the daily challenge behind him, Nuseir channeled his energy into Nas Academy, an online education platform teaching video creation, storytelling, and entrepreneurship. The mission evolved from telling stories to empowering others to tell theirs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you want to know about Nas Daily, his 1-minute videos, and the 1,000-day challenge.

Why does Nas Daily make 1-minute videos?
Nas Daily chose the 1-minute format because he believes every story in the world can be told in 60 seconds or less. The strict time constraint forces clarity and emotional precision, stripping away filler to deliver the core human story as efficiently as possible. This format proved perfectly suited for social media, where short-form content thrives. The discipline of condensing complex cultural narratives into one minute became Nas's signature and the foundation of his entire brand.
How many consecutive daily videos did Nas Daily make?
Nas Daily created exactly 1,000 consecutive daily videos without missing a single day. The project ran from 2016 to early 2019, with each video approximately one minute long. Over those 1,000 days, he traveled to 64 countries, documented hundreds of human-interest stories, and built a following of over 40 million people on Facebook alone. The challenge required extraordinary discipline -- filming, editing, and uploading every single day regardless of location, weather, illness, or exhaustion.
What is Nas Academy?
Nas Academy is an online education platform founded by Nuseir Yassin after completing his 1,000-day video challenge. The platform offers courses taught by creators and experts on topics including video production, storytelling, personal branding, and entrepreneurship. It represents the next chapter of Nas Daily's mission: after spending 1,000 days telling stories about the world, he shifted focus to empowering others with the skills and knowledge to tell their own stories and build their own platforms.
Where is Nas Daily from and what is his real name?
Nas Daily's real name is Nuseir Yassin. He is Israeli-Palestinian, born and raised in Arraba, a small Arab town in northern Israel. He attended Harvard University on a scholarship, earning a degree in economics and computer science. After a brief career in tech at Venmo in New York, he quit to pursue full-time travel and content creation. He later made Singapore his home base, drawn to the city-state's multicultural environment and strategic location in Southeast Asia.
