About Yes Theory

Yes Theory began with a simple but radical idea: what happens when you say yes to everything that scares you? Founded by Thomas Brag, Ammar Kandil, and Matt Dajer while studying at McGill University in Montreal, the group turned their philosophy of seeking discomfort into one of the most inspiring travel and adventure channels on YouTube.

The channel's origin story is as unconventional as its content. Thomas, originally from Sweden, Ammar from Egypt, and Matt from the United States found each other through a shared desire to break free from the predictable routines of university life. They started filming challenges on campus -- approaching strangers, doing things that terrified them, and documenting the results. The raw, authentic energy of those early videos resonated immediately, and their audience grew rapidly.

What sets Yes Theory apart from other travel channels is their focus on human connection over destination showcasing. Their most iconic videos involve asking complete strangers to travel with them to random countries, sleeping on the rooftops of major retail stores, swimming across international borders, and skydiving with people they met minutes before jumping out of the plane. Each video is built around the core principle that growth happens outside your comfort zone.

The "Seek Discomfort" motto has transcended YouTube to become a global lifestyle brand. The clothing line, which features the phrase prominently, has become a symbol for a generation that values experiences over possessions. Fans wear it as a daily reminder to push past their fears and embrace the unknown. The brand has collaborated with major names and expanded into a full lifestyle ecosystem.

Their travel content is uniquely immersive because it never feels scripted or polished to the point of losing authenticity. When Thomas knocks on a stranger's door in a remote village or when the team challenges themselves to cross a country using only the kindness of strangers, the audience sees genuine human interactions unfold in real time. They have traveled to over 50 countries, but their videos focus less on tourist attractions and more on the people, stories, and unexpected adventures that arise when you say yes.

Yes Theory has also tackled major personal challenges, including Thomas's open discussions about anxiety and mental health, Ammar's journey of reconnecting with his roots, and the team's various physical endurance challenges. With over 8 million subscribers and billions of views, they have proven that the most compelling travel content comes not from showing the most beautiful places, but from showing the most beautiful moments of human connection and personal growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who are the members of Yes Theory?
Yes Theory was founded by Thomas Brag (from Sweden), Ammar Kandil (from Egypt), and Matt Dajer (from the United States). They met while studying at McGill University in Montreal, Canada. Thomas remains the primary host and face of the channel, while all three continue to contribute to the brand and content creation.
What does Seek Discomfort mean?
Seek Discomfort is Yes Theory's motto and lifestyle brand. It embodies the philosophy that the best experiences in life come from stepping outside your comfort zone. The phrase has become a clothing line, a global community, and a movement inspiring millions of people to embrace fear, say yes to new experiences, and grow through challenges.
What are Yes Theory's most popular videos?
Yes Theory's most popular videos include asking strangers to travel to random countries, spending 24 hours in extreme locations, swimming across international borders, sleeping on store rooftops, and various skydiving and extreme sports challenges. Their content consistently earns millions of views by combining genuine human connection with boundary-pushing adventures across the globe.
Where is Yes Theory based?
Yes Theory is based in Los Angeles, California. The founders originally met at McGill University in Montreal, Canada, and relocated to LA to pursue content creation full-time. They travel extensively from their LA base, filming adventures in countries around the world while maintaining their studio and production operations in Southern California.
