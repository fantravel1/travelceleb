About Sailing La Vagabonde

Riley Whitelum and Elayna Carausu are an Australian couple who left conventional life behind to sail the world's oceans on their catamaran. What started as a daring adventure by two young travelers has evolved into one of the most popular sailing channels on YouTube, inspiring millions to consider a life less ordinary on the open water.

Riley, originally from Australia, had been sailing solo for years before meeting Elayna, a musician and free spirit, in Greece. Their instant connection led to a decision that would change both of their lives: Elayna joined Riley on his sailboat, and together they set off across the Mediterranean with little more than a shared love of the sea and a camera to document the journey. Those early videos, raw and unfiltered, captured the magic of two people falling in love while navigating the world's oceans.

Their channel documents everything from the breathtaking beauty of remote Pacific atolls and Caribbean anchorages to the raw reality of overnight ocean passages, equipment failures, and storms at sea. They have completed multiple major ocean crossings including the Atlantic and Pacific, visiting over 50 countries entirely by sailboat. Along the way, they have encountered whales, dolphins, sharks, and some of the most pristine marine environments on the planet.

What sets Sailing La Vagabonde apart is their commitment to sustainable living. Their catamaran is equipped with solar panels, a wind generator, and a watermaker, allowing them to live almost entirely off the grid. They are vocal advocates for ocean conservation, frequently highlighting the impact of plastic pollution and climate change on the marine environments they traverse. Their partnership with organizations focused on ocean cleanup has brought environmental awareness to millions of viewers.

As their family has grown to include children born into the sailing life, Riley and Elayna have shown the world that raising kids aboard a sailboat is not only possible but deeply enriching. Their children are growing up surrounded by nature, learning about marine biology firsthand, and experiencing cultures across the globe -- all from the deck of their floating home.

Sailing Routes

The major ocean passages and cruising grounds that define Sailing La Vagabonde's journey around the world.

⛺️

Atlantic Crossing

Their legendary transatlantic passage from the Canary Islands to the Caribbean. Weeks of open ocean sailing, starlit night watches, dolphin encounters, and the raw thrill of making landfall after thousands of nautical miles with nothing but wind power.

🌊

Pacific Ocean

The ultimate sailing challenge -- crossing the vast Pacific Ocean through French Polynesia, Tonga, Fiji, and beyond. Remote atolls, pristine reefs, and the most isolated waters on Earth tested their seamanship and rewarded them with unmatched beauty.

🏝

Mediterranean

Where it all began -- sailing the crystal waters of Greece, Turkey, Croatia, and Italy. Ancient harbors, turquoise coves, and the romance of Mediterranean island-hopping that first brought Riley and Elayna together on the open sea.

🌴

Caribbean

Island-hopping through the Caribbean chain from the Windward to the Leeward Islands. Turquoise anchorages, vibrant reef snorkeling, lively island culture, and the perfect trade winds that make this a sailor's paradise.

🐋

Indian Ocean

Navigating the Indian Ocean through the Maldives, Sri Lanka, and the coast of East Africa. Whale shark encounters, pristine diving, and the challenge of monsoon seasons across one of the world's most dynamic sailing regions.

The Journey

From a chance meeting in Greece to sailing the world's oceans with a growing family.

2014 - Greece
Riley and Elayna Meet
Riley had been sailing solo across the Mediterranean when he met Elayna, a young Australian musician traveling through Greece. Their connection was instant. Elayna joined Riley aboard his small monohull, and together they began documenting their adventures on YouTube -- the first chapter of Sailing La Vagabonde.
2015 - Mediterranean
Building an Audience
Sailing through Turkey, Croatia, Italy, and the Greek islands, Riley and Elayna found their rhythm as both sailing partners and content creators. Their honest, unpolished videos about life on a small sailboat resonated with viewers hungry for authentic adventure content. The channel began to grow rapidly.
2016 - Atlantic Ocean
First Atlantic Crossing
The crossing that put them on the map. Setting sail from the Canary Islands, Riley and Elayna spent weeks crossing the Atlantic Ocean to reach the Caribbean. The raw footage of night watches, mid-ocean storms, and the emotional landfall captivated millions of viewers and established them as serious ocean sailors.
2018 - Upgrade
New Catamaran
With their channel growing beyond a million subscribers, Riley and Elayna upgraded from their small monohull to a Lagoon catamaran. The larger vessel provided more living space, better stability, and the solar panel capacity to support their off-grid, sustainable sailing lifestyle for the long haul.
2019 - At Sea
First Child Born
Riley and Elayna welcomed their first child into the sailing life. The baby joined the crew aboard the catamaran, adding a beautiful new dimension to their content. Viewers watched as the couple navigated the joys and challenges of parenting on the open ocean, proving that family and adventure are not mutually exclusive.
2020 - Pacific Ocean
Pacific Crossing with Baby
One of their most ambitious passages yet -- crossing the Pacific Ocean as a young family. From French Polynesia through the South Pacific islands, they sailed thousands of miles with their baby aboard, exploring some of the most remote and pristine islands on the planet.
2022 - Present
Growing Family, Growing Adventures
With their family continuing to grow, Riley and Elayna show no signs of slowing down. They have now visited over 50 countries entirely by sailboat, crossed multiple oceans, and built a community of over 2 million subscribers who follow their sustainable, family-centered sailing lifestyle around the world.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Sailing La Vagabonde about?
Sailing La Vagabonde is a YouTube channel run by Australian couple Riley Whitelum and Elayna Carausu. They document their life sailing around the world on a catamaran with their children, sharing ocean crossings, remote island discoveries, marine wildlife encounters, and the daily realities of living on a sailboat full-time. With over 2 million subscribers, they are one of the most popular sailing channels on YouTube and pioneers of the sailing vlog genre.
How much does it cost to sail the world like Sailing La Vagabonde?
The costs of sailing the world vary significantly depending on the boat, equipment, and lifestyle. Riley and Elayna have been transparent about costs: their first monohull was purchased for around $6,000, while their current catamaran was a significant upgrade. Monthly cruising costs typically range from $2,000 to $5,000 covering food, maintenance, marina fees, and insurance. They fund their lifestyle through YouTube ad revenue, Patreon supporters, and brand partnerships, proving that sailing can be more affordable than conventional living in many parts of the world.
Is it safe to sail the ocean with young children?
Riley and Elayna have demonstrated that ocean sailing with children can be done safely with proper preparation. They use jacklines, harnesses, and netting to secure the boat for kids. Their children wear life jackets on deck at all times, and the family carries extensive safety equipment including an EPIRB, life raft, satellite communication, and medical supplies. They carefully plan passages around weather windows and avoid sailing during dangerous seasons. Many cruising families around the world successfully raise children aboard sailboats with proper precautions.
What boat does Sailing La Vagabonde sail?
Sailing La Vagabonde currently sails a Lagoon 42 catamaran, which they upgraded to after starting their journey on a much smaller monohull. The catamaran provides more living space for their growing family, better stability for ocean crossings, and solar panels for sustainable energy generation. They have equipped the boat with watermakers, solar power systems, and other off-grid technology to minimize their environmental footprint while living at sea full-time.
